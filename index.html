PPC Overhaul

Children's Clothing

Overview

We worked with a children’s clothing brand that was organically generating 7-figures annually on Amazon. They sold a variety of clothing and accessories for children ranging from newborns – 6 years of age. Though they were thriving, their PPC campaigns were in shambles and not performing as they should.

Challenges

In total, there were 300 campaigns spread across 2,000+ ASINS – these campaigns followed no best practices and were in such a state of disarray that it was a seemingly impossible to task navigate through them all. For context, the campaigns had no naming structure, unrelated ASINS were being paired with one another, and 100s of keywords were being stuffed into individual ad groups targeting 100s of different ASINS.

Solution

To resolve these issues, we first conducted a complete ad account audit. We identified low, middle, and top performing campaigns and segmented them out accordingly. We then took a deep dive into analyzing why these campaigns were performing at the level they were; with these insights in hand, we were forced to close out a large majority of these campaigns due to how inefficient it would be to go through each one and correct the various problems.

With the insights obtained from the audit we then focused on developing a long-term strategy that would ensure future campaigns followed a strict naming structure and a proper ad hierarchy. We then sought to create campaigns focused on establishing brand dominance for specific keywords and top performing products, along with developing campaigns focused on boosting sales of underperforming products.

Results

From the end of 2018 to the end of 2019 we were able to help this brand generate over $1million in ad revenue with a total spend of $226k and a ROAS of 460%.

MISIG

PPC Development and Management

Overview

MISIG, a PGA-affiliated brand, offers a 1-of-a-kind stretch device aimed at helping golfers improve their swing and overall mobility. With thousands of units sold, MISIG is a leader in their industry. MISIG reached out to us to help identify and resolve problems they were experiencing in their Amazon AD account.

Challenges Faced

The challenges we faced included a low ROAS and progressively decreasing sales via their paid initiatives. The ad account lacked basic organization, ad structure, and it was apparent that there was a fundamental misunderstanding on how to properly develop and optimize campaigns. While MISIG performed well during their initial launch, sales began to taper off within the 4th week of being on the platform.

Solutions

To identify and rectify the problems plaguing their account we first conducted an account audit. This allowed us to identify low, mid, and top performing campaigns. Post-audit we were able to take the insights gathered and develop a comprehensive strategy that we would use over the term of our working relationship. This strategy included Keyword Optimization, Keyword Bid Best Practices, Campaign Structure Optimization, Budget Allocation, Continuous Monitoring and Iteration, and Competitor Analysis.

Results

By following our established procedures and closely monitoring the brands ad account, we were able to achieve $123k in total ad revenue, with a spend of $19k, equaling out to a ROAS of 620%,

  • Client: MISIG
  • Category: Golf Equipment

Fitness Brand

Amazon Brand Management

Overview

We were contracted to work with an Amazon exclusive brand specializing in fitness equipment to drive revenue growth through PPC campaigns and listing optimization strategies. With this brand we focused on the UK, German, Spanish, Italian, North American, and French markets

Challenges Faced

Though the brand was doing well organically they had not yet established PPC campaigns or listing optimization efforts; this effectively hindered their growth and left revenue on the table. We were tasked with taking on the account and developing strategies that would enable the brand to increase their revenue and thrive in the long-term.

Solutions

We worked with the brand in developing a deeper understanding of their products and current customer base. The next step was to conduct competitor and keyword research to identify low competition keywords that we could establish dominance over. Once we had established the initial keywords we would be targeting, we developed an initial 12-month strategy where we would deploy said strategy and scale the brand through PPC and listing optimization efforts.

Results

From September 21’– May 23’ we were able to generate €776k in total revenue over a 20-month period. With €200k being associated with PPC campaigns, we achieved a ROAS of 7,000% across all our campaigns.

  • Client: Fitness Brand
  • Category: Fitness Equipment

Tutublue Brand Management

Re-Launch of Swimwear Brand on Amazon

Overview

Tutublue is a UV protection swimwear brand that provides a variety of products that help men, women, and children partake in outdoor activities while staying protected from harmful UVA and UVB rays. The brand sought out our help after having entrusted their account to an agency who had ruined their presence on the platform and left them with no support.

Challenges Faced

The issues that plagued the account included a complete loss of brand presence, Tutublue was no longer ranking for their brand keywords, excessive inventory of non-converting products, poorly created product listings, no utilization of A+ content, poorly designed store front, ineffective PPC campaigns, and FBA order creation errors causing inaccurate shipment counts.

Solutions

In order to address these challenges, we conducted an inventory audit to identify top and underperforming products and to create removal orders to mitigate excessive inventory; followed by a product listing audits to identify areas for improvement. We then created the Amazon store front, A+ content, and performed an audit on their PPC campaigns, followed up by the strategic optimization of the ad account.

Results

Our contract was set to last from May to August 2022 – during that time we grew revenue to $1,174 in the month of May and $8,325 in monthly revenue by August.

  • Client: Tutublue
  • Category: Swimwear
